# Pricing Process Vocabulary

## Canonical Terms and Definitions

### Core Pricing Concepts

**Base Price**  
The fundamental price for a product before any adjustments, discounts, or surcharges are applied. Also known as list price or standard price.

**Price Point**  
A specific price level set for a product or service, often one of several options in a tiered pricing structure.

**Pricing Model**  
A systematic approach or methodology used to determine prices, such as cost-plus, value-based, or competitive pricing.

**Pricing Strategy**  
High-level approach defining how an organization positions its prices to achieve business objectives and competitive advantage.

**Price Elasticity**  
Measure of how demand for a product changes in response to price changes, typically expressed as a percentage.

### Pricing Methodologies

**Cost-Plus Pricing**  
Pricing method that adds a predetermined markup percentage to the total cost of producing a product or service.

**Value-Based Pricing**  
Pricing strategy that sets prices primarily based on the perceived value to the customer rather than on cost or competition.

**Competitive Pricing**  
Pricing approach that sets prices based on competitor pricing levels and market positioning.

**Dynamic Pricing**  
Pricing strategy that adjusts prices in real-time based on market conditions, demand, and other variables.

**Tiered Pricing**  
Pricing structure offering multiple price levels or packages with different features or value propositions.

### Financial Terms

**Gross Margin**  
The difference between revenue and cost of goods sold, expressed as a percentage of revenue.

**Price Variance**  
The difference between actual selling price and standard or target price.

**Price Realization**  
The actual average selling price achieved in the market, accounting for discounts and negotiations.

**Contribution Margin**  
Revenue minus variable costs, representing the amount available to cover fixed costs and profit.

**Price Sensitivity**  
The degree to which price changes affect customer purchasing behavior and demand levels.

### Approval and Governance

**Approval Threshold**  
Predetermined criteria (such as discount percentage or total value) that trigger required approval workflows.

**Pricing Authority**  
The level of organizational authority required to approve specific pricing decisions.

**Price Exception**  
A pricing decision that deviates from standard pricing policies and requires special approval.

**Escalation Path**  
The sequence of approvals required for pricing decisions that exceed normal authority levels.

### System and Process Terms

**Price Master**  
Central repository containing all current and historical pricing information for products and services.

**Price Calculation Engine**  
System component responsible for executing pricing algorithms and generating price recommendations.

**Price Publication**  
The process of making approved prices available in customer-facing systems and sales channels.

**Price Synchronization**  
The process of ensuring price consistency across multiple systems and sales channels.

### Performance and Analytics

**Price Performance**  
Measurement of how well prices achieve intended business objectives such as revenue, margin, or market share.

**Price Analytics**  
Analysis of pricing data to understand performance, trends, and optimization opportunities.

**Price Optimization**  
Systematic process of adjusting prices to maximize business objectives within defined constraints.

**Win Rate**  
Percentage of sales opportunities won, often analyzed in relation to pricing competitiveness.

### Customer and Market Terms

**Customer Segment**  
Distinct group of customers with similar characteristics, often with differentiated pricing approaches.

**Market Positioning**  
Where a product's price places it relative to competitors in terms of premium, value, or economy positioning.

**Price Transparency**  
The degree to which pricing information is openly available and understandable to customers.

**Price Sensitivity Analysis**  
Study of how price changes affect customer behavior and market demand.

### Operational Terms

**Price Effective Date**  
The date when a price becomes active and available for use in customer transactions.

**Price Expiration**  
The date when a price is no longer valid and must be replaced or renewed.

**Price Override**  
Manual adjustment to system-generated prices, typically requiring approval.

**Price Validation**  
Process of verifying that calculated prices meet business rules and quality standards.
